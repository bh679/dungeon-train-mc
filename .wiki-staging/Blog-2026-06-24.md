## New Carriages

### Witch Carriage *(v0.360.0)*

A new enemy carriage haunted by a witch joins the train roster. The huskskel carriage also received improved loot chests — randomised pools covering weapons, armour, and food instead of fixed drops.

- New witch carriage with a witch mob
- Huskskel chests now use randomised loot pools (weapons, armour, food)

### More Carriage Variants *(v0.359.0)*

Five new variants added to existing carriage types: two additional statue carriages, a second worship shrine, and two new shop carriages (a smithing variant and a leatherworker variant). A wither barrel loot table was also added.

### Shop, Maze, Library & Shrine Carriages *(v0.358.0)*

A full batch of new carriage layouts:

- **Shop carriages** — an archer stall and a cleric
- **Maze rooms** — two new layouts plus a doors room
- **Library carriages** — a two-lane library and a book-door room
- **Feature carriages** — a statue, a grave, a worship shrine, and a wither room

## Gameplay

### Item Stats Scale with Difficulty *(v0.358.0)*

Weapons and armour found deeper in the run now hit harder and hold up better. Each difficulty level adds a flat bonus to the item's primary stat — attack damage for weapons, armour value for armour — with axes getting a little extra.

- +0.10 attack damage / armour per difficulty level
- +0.15 per level for axes
- Applies to chest loot, container and vase loot, and PlayerMob equipment
- Regular mob gear and secondary stats (attack speed, toughness) are unchanged

### New Advancement: Hell on Rails *(v0.357.0)*

Ride the train deep into the Nether band to earn the new *Hell on Rails* advancement. It triggers once you're well inside the netherrack core — not at the first glimpse of red sky — and sits just before *Voided Warranty* in the advancement tree.

### New Quiet Rule: Mending Gear by Combining *(v0.355.0)*

The *Quiet Rules of the Train* book can now turn up a new rule hinting that worn-down weapons and armour can be mended by combining two of the same item, and that the left-hand item's stats carry over to the result.

## World Generation

### Longer Nether Crossing — 5,000 Blocks *(v0.357.0)*

The real-Nether core of the train's journey has been extended from 400 blocks to 5,000 — matching the length of the End-island stretch. The train now spends a substantial time crossing genuine Nether terrain (red sky, netherrack, all five Nether biomes, lava lakes on pillars) rather than a brief flicker.

## Bug Fixes

### No More Basalt on the Nether Tracks *(v0.360.0)*

Basalt from basalt-delta stretches could end up sitting on the rails, the bed, or the centre line — most stubbornly right at the leading edge of newly-generated track. The corridor cleanup now re-sweeps neighbours on chunk load and re-lays the real track template over anything that landed there.

### Nether Tunnel Stairs No Longer Waterlogged *(v0.359.0)*

The train tunnel and its pillar staircases now correctly stamp as dry through the Nether band. Previously, terrain water could seep into the stone-brick stairs, slabs, and walls of the corridor. Fixed by setting `IGNORE_WATERLOGGING` on both worldgen and runtime stamps.

### Train Villagers Now Have Unique Names and Trades *(v0.357.0)*

Villagers riding the train previously spawned as identical twins — the same name and the same trades in every carriage of a given type. Each villager now gets a unique randomly-generated name and a freshly rolled set of trades, while preserving the carriage's profession.

### Beds Only Explode in the Real Nether Core *(v0.356.0)*

Beds were incorrectly exploding in the overworld's Nether biome zone. They now only blow up inside the actual Nether core, matching vanilla behaviour. Sleeping also now shows a clearer message explaining that your respawn point travels with the train.

### Pause/Resume Fix Temporarily Reverted *(v0.355.0)*

The v0.354.0 fix that kept you aboard the train across a singleplayer pause introduced a new issue and has been temporarily rolled back. The older trade-off returns: pausing mid-ride can again leave you behind as the train moves on. A proper fix is in progress.
