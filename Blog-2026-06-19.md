# Dev Log — Week of 13–19 June 2026

A massive week — the death screen was rebuilt from the ground up, the mod climbed from v0.293 to v0.327, and dozens of features shipped across every part of the game. Highlights: a cinematic train intro, a fully narrated paginated death screen with ride-photo backdrops and a photo gallery, a Free Play mode for creative runs, 30+ new advancements, enemy difficulty scaling, an Ender Chest that follows you across worlds, a cinematographer spectator mode, and a Sable 2.0.2 physics update that halves worst-case lag spikes.

## Sunday 14 June

*Versions v0.294.0 – v0.303.0*

### Cinematic spawn-on-train intro

You now begin each new world aboard the train. On first arrival a short cinematic sweeps up from the ground to reveal your train before handing you the controls — press Space at any time to skip.

### Enemies scale with difficulty level

Train enemies now spawn based on the current difficulty. Each spawn egg carries a difficulty range, so the early game stays gentle (small slimes, basic mobs) while tougher foes like husks, bogged and vindicators ramp in only as you travel deeper. When an enemy is out of its range, that spot fills with an in-range enemy instead — carriages and cages are never left empty.

- Easy mobs (small slimes) gated to the opening levels; tougher mobs appear only at higher difficulty
- Editors can set a per-egg min/max difficulty band in the block-variant menu

### Five train-traversal advancements

Hop down onto the tracks, wander off, find your way back aboard, and discover the bridge-pillar staircases and tunnel stairwells along the route:

- **Track Record** — set foot on the rails below the train
- **Off the Rails** — leave the train and tracks behind for the open world
- **Back on Track** — climb aboard again after getting off
- **Taking the Stairs** — use a bridge-pillar staircase
- **Beneath the Candy Kingdom** — enter a tunnel stairwell

### Carriage spawn-egg mobs arrive armed

Pillagers carry a crossbow (with a chance at the ominous banner); skeletons and bogged carry a bow. At higher difficulty, ranged mobs have their bow or crossbow enchanted instead of receiving a replacement melee weapon.

### Keep Inventory now carries your gear to the next world

With Keep Inventory on, dying and starting a New World brings your whole inventory and XP along — and the new world keeps the rule on, so gear and XP chain across every run.

### Compatible Terrain mode + Distant Horizons & Tectonic support

A new **Compatible Terrain** option lets Dungeon Train generate vanilla-style overworlds so terrain mods like Tectonic and Terralith — and Distant Horizons' long-range views — work with the train. Off by default; switch on in Dungeon Train settings before creating a world. The modpack now offers Distant Horizons and Tectonic as optional add-ons.

### Leatherworker shop carriage

A leatherworker's stall now rides the train. Stocked with leather, the full leather-armour set, a complete rainbow of dyes, emeralds, a cauldron, a potion and a water bucket.

### Advancements keybind reminder in chat

Earn a Dungeon Train advancement before you've ever opened the advancements screen and a short conductor-style message points you at the key that opens it — using your actual (rebindable) keybind. Stops showing once you open the screen.

### Armor stands as randomized carriage contents

Carriage cells can now feature an armor stand wearing a rolled set of armor, adding variety to what you find aboard.

### Auto-publish CurseForge modpack after each release

Dungeon Train now ships as a CurseForge modpack that updates automatically ~15 minutes after every mod release, bundling the matching mod build plus the tested Sable version.

### Fixes

- **Advancements** now only earnable in survival or adventure mode (editor advancements still unlock in creative)
- **Piglins** no longer wrongly become magma cubes outside the Nether
- **Reliable spawn** on the train's front deck — no more falling under on join or respawn
- **Empty enemy spots** no longer leave a stray block when the difficulty-appropriate mob isn't available yet

## Monday 15 June

*Versions v0.304.0 – v0.310.0*

### Gentler opening: mob-free start, then slimes, then normal

New runs ease in over three stages. The first stretch (~10 carriages) spawns no hostile mobs — meet villagers and grab starter loot in peace. The next stretch (~15 carriages) spawns only slimes for a gentle warm-up. Normal mobs and the full difficulty curve resume after that. Fully tunable in `dungeontrain-server.toml`.

### Meet, befriend, or lay to rest your own echo

Three new advancements follow reunions with your own echo — a passenger reincarnated from one of your past lives:

- **Hello, Old Soul** — come within two blocks of your own echo
- **Old Friends, New Life** — befriend your own echo
- **Lay to Rest** — kill your own echo

### Archways fade between elevated track pillars

Where the track climbs onto tall support pillars — crossing ravines, rivers, and oceans — the pillars now fade toward each other with stepped archways instead of standing as bare posts. The arches are built from each pillar's own stone, so themed pillars arch in their own material.

### Everything Burrito: earn every other advancement

A new challenge advancement — **Everything Burrito** — unlocks once you've earned every other Dungeon Train advancement (editor achievements aside). It tracks the list dynamically, so it always means 100% even as future updates add more.

### New advancements — Know The Rules, ride-time milestones, befriending echoes

- **Know The Rules** — read every Quiet Rule of the train (replaces the old ??? tooltip); Respect The Rules now nests beneath it
- **Settling In** (ten hours aboard) and **I Live Here Now** (twenty-four hours) now spell out how long you need to stay

### More Quiet Rules of the Train

Ten new entries: reaching the maker via @Dev, a rule that reveals your own advancements key inline, and seven tips on befriending (or surviving) fellow adventurers.

### New decor carriages — house, tree, cherry blossom, broken roof

A new features content set: house, tree, and cherry-blossom builds appear inside carriage types, with loot chests in the tree and cherry-blossom variants. Cracked carriages gain a new broken-roof look.

### Type @Dev in chat — autocomplete + highlight

Typing `@Dev` or `@brennanhatton` in chat now autocompletes and turns yellow as you type, with the deeper Discord wiring bundled (off by default until a server owner enables it). Pairs with the new Quiet Rule about reaching the maker.

### Death-screen feedback survey

A **Give Feedback** button on the death screen opens a short survey — rate your run 0–10 with an optional comment, then answer how difficulty progression feels. Answers post to the community Discord. Walks questions one at a time and never re-asks one you've already answered.

### Editor welcome message

Opening the in-game editor now greets you with a short welcome pointing out what the editor can do and how to reach Brennan for help. Includes a live "last seen on Discord" note so creators know if a quick reply is likely.

### Modpack additions

- **AppleSkin** (food/saturation overlay) now on by default
- **Mouse Tweaks** (inventory quality-of-life) offered as an optional add-on
- **Jade** block & item tooltips offered as an optional add-on

### Fixes

- **Players no longer spawn under the train** on busy world loads
- **Pause menu is now clickable** during the intro cutscene (previously buttons were frozen)

## Tuesday 16 June

*Versions v0.311.0 – v0.315.0*

### All advancements now persist across worlds

Every advancement you earn — vanilla Minecraft, Dungeon Train, and other mods — now follows you into every new world. Advancements earned in existing worlds are absorbed automatically on next load. Recipe-book unlocks remain per-world.

### Narrative death screen

Dying on the train now opens a paginated, narrated retrospective instead of the plain vanilla screen. The Dungeon Train reflects on your fall, your deeds, the cargo you carried, all your lives, and the platform ahead — run and lifetime stats woven in, lore that adapts to how you died. Page through with Continue, then board anew or leave the line.

### New advancement: The Great Beyond

Complete the death-screen feedback survey to earn **The Great Beyond**. Once you've answered every survey question, it unlocks. Comes with a new `/feedback` command to open the survey any time.

### Time-aboard & distance-ridden advancements

Seven new advancements reward the long haul. Stay aboard for an hour or two without dying, and rack up kilometres ridden both within a single life and across every run you've taken. Branch off Dungeon Train Explorer in the advancements screen.

### Tagging @dev shows Brennan's recent online status

Tag `@dev` or `@brennanhatton` in chat and the game shows how recently Brennan was online on Discord — "online right now" or "online 5 minutes ago". Only shown within the last 30 minutes, rate-limited to once every 5 minutes.

### Replay the intro cinematic on demand

Run `/dungeontrain cinematic` (or `/dt cinematic`) any time to watch the spawn cinematic again. Use `/dt cinematic current` to start from exactly where you're looking.

### Carriage # and difficulty on Discord advancement posts

Earn an advancement and the community Discord now shows which carriage you were riding and your difficulty level at that moment.

### New book: "All the Creator Asks"

A new random book — a fallen adventurer's reverent testament treating the feedback survey as a holy rite of the afterlife. Eight short variants found in chests, on bookshelves, and at narrative lecterns.

### Advancement: Over the Top

New advancement: **Over the Top** — run across the roof of one carriage group without dropping inside. Sits before Skip the Grind in the tree.

### Fixes & adjustments

- **Fewer trees** in carriage contents (reduced spawn weight)
- **"Reboarder"** advancement now fires when passengers fall off the side or onto the tracks, not only clean knock-offs
- **Vanilla movement tutorial** (WASD/look/jump hints) no longer appears in new Dungeon Train worlds

## Wednesday 17 June

*Version v0.316.0*

### TrainCinematographer mode

New spectator mode for filming dungeon runs. Enter with `/dungeontrain cinematographer`. Nearby doors auto-open as you approach, press C to replay the cinematic intro, and the DT branch HUD hides while active. Only actual doors open — trapdoors, fence gates and barrels stay put so set dressing is preserved.

### Persistent Ender Chest across runs

The Ender Chest now persists between worlds — items you store survive your death and carry into the next run. Survival and creative inventories are kept separate. Shipped as the standalone **Ender Chest Persistence** mod, embedded in the DT jar (no separate install needed).

## Thursday 18 June

*Versions v0.317.0 – v0.322.0*

### Photos of your ride behind the death screen

The train now quietly snaps third-person photos of your journey and shows them as the backdrop on the death screen — a recap of your run, with the most fitting shot behind each page.

- Photos only snap when there's an unobstructed line to your character; inside walled carriages the camera finds an aisle angle or skips until one opens
- Photos space themselves across the journey (not bunched at the start) and never repeat two pages in a row
- Only fires when the game has performance headroom — no hitches while you're lagging

### Portrait of the traveller you befriended or killed

The death screen's deeds page shows an animated full-body portrait of a fellow traveller from your run — the player-mob you befriended (left) or the one you killed (right) — with their name and gear. Friendly travellers glance around and crouch-bob; ones you killed stare you down and raise their shield.

### Cause of death on the death screen

The first page title now shows your actual cause of death in second person — "You fell from a high place", "You were slain by a zombie", "You blew up" — instead of the generic "the fall".

### Cinematic death-screen page transitions

The death screen now turns pages with a slow fade through black. Text and overlay fade out, the next page fades in, and the ride photo behind it rises gently out of black. Press Continue whenever you're ready.

### Cinematographer clear-view: see through walls

Cinematographer mode gains a clear-view sub-mode: blocks directly in front of the camera turn invisible so walls and clutter don't block the shot, then quietly return as you pan away.

### Free Play for creative & cheat runs

Playing in creative, spectator, or using cheat commands no longer blocks advancements — you keep earning them live. The run becomes **Free Play**: a confirmation lets you back out first, a permanent spyglass status effect marks the run, and anything earned won't be saved to your profile or count toward global stats. Start a new world for a fresh ranked run.

### Sable physics updated to 2.0.2

Updated from Sable 1.2.1 to 2.0.2. Worst-case server lag spikes are roughly halved, and the long-standing save-and-quit hang (Sable #679) is fixed. API-compatible — no gameplay changes. Full benchmark notes: [Compatibility](Compatibility#sable-202mc1211).

### Biome-diversity advancements

Ride the train through a variety of biomes in a single life to earn four new advancements — from your first handful of biomes all the way to seeing every kind of terrain the world has to offer.

### Death screen: lectern stories count as books

Stories you read at narrative lecterns now count toward the "Books read" tally on the death-screen run summary. Your very first death also gets its own bespoke lines on the "all your lives" and "the platform" pages instead of the generic veteran-rider send-off.

### Feedback survey updated

The survey's second question is now open-ended: "If you could change one thing about the mod, what would it be?" — a free-text box instead of a 0–10 rating.

### Modpack

- **FerriteCore** bundled and on by default (deduplicates block-state/model data, lower RAM)
- **ModernFix** bundled and on by default (faster launch and world-load, less memory)

### Content

- Zombie carriages spawn more zombies, lit with corner torches
- Book carriage room layout refreshed
- Biome-family "discovered" action-bar notification removed (exploration advancements still track)

## Friday 19 June

*Versions v0.323.0 – v0.327.0*

### Ride-photo gallery on the death screen

The death screen's final page now has a **Photos** button that opens a gallery of every ride photo taken during the run. Browse them all and save any (or all) to your screenshots folder. Photos also offload to disk during quiet moments so long runs stay memory-efficient.

### Advancement hints replace the ??? tooltip

Unearned advancements now show a hint or riddle instead of a blank "???". Covers **48 Dungeon Train advancements** across riding, reading, exploring and befriending — the most spoiler-heavy ones deliberately stay a mystery.

### Advancements on the death-screen cargo page

The cargo page now shows the Dungeon Train advancements you earned this life — each in its real advancement frame. Loot-container and book tallies move to the gear row as chest/book icons (hover for labels). A button opens the full advancements screen.

### Uncraftable arrows pack an escalating punch

The effectless tipped arrows you scavenge from vases and chests now carry a real offensive potion that escalates every 50 carriages:

> Weakness → Slowness → Poison → Harming → Poison II → Harming II

Every arrow found within a stretch shares the same effect, then steps up at the next. Loot containers you place yourself scale to your travelled distance too.

### PlayerMobs follow you on the train, echoes wear real skins

A PlayerMob that has come to love you now leaves its route to ride in your carriage. Reincarnated "Echo of \<name\>" mobs now wear the real player's skin. The skin pool grew to 68 with 30 new recognizable skins — appearing ~40% of the time.

### Modpack overhaul

- **Advancement Plaques** on by default — fancy animated plaques instead of vanilla toast
- **AmbientSounds** on by default — immersive ambient sound engine (client-side only)
- AppleSkin, FerriteCore and ModernFix now correctly on by default
- Mouse Tweaks, Jade, Distant Horizons (2.x) and Tectonic bundled as one-click opt-ins

### Discord chat-relay consent card + /chatconnect

A new title-screen card explains the optional Discord chat relay and lets you opt in or out. A new `/chatconnect` command toggles it in-game.

### Fixes

- **Space/Enter** no longer accidentally advance the death screen (click Continue to proceed)
- **Pause-menu buttons** (Options, Advancements, Statistics, Feedback) now work during the intro cinematic
- **Feedback survey** Continue button now advances past questions whether or not you answer them
- **'Collecting Stories' hint** text updated: "The lecterns hold the key."
- **Breaking a narrative lectern** now drops a normal lectern and its story book
- **Stray command block** removed from the vindicator carriage

### Content

- **Echo lore books** — two new echo-themed variants in Quiet Rules of the Train, two new past-life observations in Adventurer's Field Notes
- **Field Notes: passenger observation** — an anonymous adventurer wonders whether the passengers are truly alive, and notes that food, armour, or tools make the fastest allies
- **More community names** in the item-name pool (AIN 0.44.0: JJ, Freshboii, VidTu, DanMizu, Varocraft25)
