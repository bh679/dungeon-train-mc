## World Generation

### The world breaks apart — repeating Void and End-island bands ([#533](https://github.com/bh679/dungeon-train-mc/pull/533))

The headline feature of the day. As the train travels, the overworld periodically **crumbles into void**, then **reforms as real floating End islands** — chorus plants and all — before dissolving back and rebuilding into normal terrain. The whole cycle repeats forever down the line.

- **Overworld → fade → Void → End islands → Void → Overworld → repeat**
- The sky, fog, and clouds crossfade to the End's starfield over the broken stretches
- Only endermen spawn in the void/End bands; normal mobs resume in the overworld sections
- The train rides straight through — the track is preserved; the surrounding terrain erodes

The End islands are generated from the **actual End noise router**, trilinearly interpolated per block the same way the real End generates, so the islands have real shapes, real gaps, and chorus plants that cluster in patches rather than blanketing every surface.

→ [Feature: World disintegration](Feature-World-Disintegration)

### A longer, more dramatic journey — 5 k + 5 k + 10 k blocks ([#534](https://github.com/bh679/dungeon-train-mc/pull/534))

The phase lengths were scaled up dramatically. Where the original shipped with 500-block stretches of each phase, the defaults are now:

| Phase | Old | New |
|-------|-----|-----|
| Overworld before first void | 500 blocks | ~5,000 blocks |
| End-island band | 500 blocks | ~5,000 blocks |
| Overworld between cycles | 500 blocks | ~10,000 blocks |

All lengths are configurable in `config/dungeontrain-common.toml` → `[worldgen]`.

### Sky holds back while the world crumbles ([#536](https://github.com/bh679/dungeon-train-mc/pull/536))

The End-sky crossfade now **lags the terrain disintegration** on both edges: the sky stays overworld-coloured a little longer as the ground erodes, and clears back to normal before solid terrain fully returns. The lag amount is tunable via the new `disintegrationSkyFadeOffsetBlocks` config key (set to `0` to restore the old in-sync behaviour).

### End music across the disintegration void ([#537](https://github.com/bh679/dungeon-train-mc/pull/537))

As the world breaks apart, the **soundtrack crossfades to the End's theme** — the normal overworld music fades out as you approach the void, the End theme plays across the void and floating islands, and it fades back as the world reforms on the far side. The crossfade is position-driven and mirrors the sky/fog transition so sound and visuals move together.

### Leaner void/End chunk generation ([#538](https://github.com/bh679/dungeon-train-mc/pull/538), [#539](https://github.com/bh679/dungeon-train-mc/pull/539))

Two performance follow-ups shipped back-to-back:

- **#538 — Empty chunks, not delete:** Void stretches no longer build full terrain only to erase it immediately. The chunks are generated empty from the start.
- **#539 — Skip decoration pass:** Void/End core chunks no longer run the vanilla tree/ore/structure decoration pass — previously they'd generate all that and throw it away. The chunk-loading hitch crossing the disintegration band should be noticeably smaller.

## Narrative — The Aaro Family

Three stories released today complete an entire character family that has been seeding via the auto-cascade over the past week.

### Tomas Aaro: "The Walking Father" ([#527](https://github.com/bh679/dungeon-train-mc/pull/527))

A farmer leaves his dying village chasing a rumour that the train holds "what you need." Six letters, branching variants. The third of the Aaro stories — completing the family arc that began with Pip and continued through Della.

### Della and Soren Aaro: complete stories ([#535](https://github.com/bh679/dungeon-train-mc/pull/535))

**Della Aaro — "The Searching Mother"** (7 letters) and **Soren — "The Keeper of the Door"** (7 letters) both shipped in full. All three Aaro family stories are now complete and riding the cascade queue onto the train.

### A creator's plea on the death screen ([#528](https://github.com/bh679/dungeon-train-mc/pull/528))

When the death-screen feedback survey reaches its **second question**, the calm "ledger" welcome is replaced by an urgent, heartfelt plea from the creator to keep the railway alive. Seven variations are in the pool — one is drawn at random each death so it lands a little differently every time.

## Train

### Seamless backward train generation ([#532](https://github.com/bh679/dungeon-train-mc/pull/532))

Walking toward the **rear** of a moving train no longer opens permanent gaps between carriages or hits a dead-end where the train just stops. Carriages now keep generating smoothly behind you, the same way they do ahead. Also fixes a rare crash and potential save corruption that could occur as far-back carriages were unloaded and reloaded.

- No more permanent voids between carriages when walking toward the tail
- The train no longer dead-ends behind you
- Fixed rare crash / save corruption from carriage unload-and-reload

## Discord

### World info embedded on player join ([#531](https://github.com/bh679/dungeon-train-mc/pull/531))

The Discord join message now includes a **world-info block**: the DT mod version, the train's world seed, and the active mod list — useful for debugging when players ask questions or report issues.

### Developer pinged on every survey submission ([#529](https://github.com/bh679/dungeon-train-mc/pull/529))

Every feedback-survey answer submitted now privately pings the developer in Discord so suggestions are seen immediately. The Free Play notice no longer pings to reduce noise.

### Echo encounter stories route to the public death feed ([#524](https://github.com/bh679/dungeon-train-mc/pull/524))

The remote-echo encounter story now appears in the **public death-report channel** alongside death manifests, instead of the live community feed. It keeps its distinct greyish-blue embed bar so it still reads apart from a normal death post.

## Fixes

### Short abandoned runs stay off the public feed ([#522](https://github.com/bh679/dungeon-train-mc/pull/522))

Bailing out early via "Abandon This Run" before reaching 20 carriages no longer broadcasts to the public death feed — only runs that actually went somewhere get posted. Your own run summary and death recap are unchanged.

### CurseForge changelogs now carry the real release notes ([#530](https://github.com/bh679/dungeon-train-mc/pull/530))

CurseForge only ever shows the newest file's changelog, and the steady stream of automated bundle updates kept pushing the curated notes out of view. Those notes are now carried forward on every cascade tick so they stay visible on the CurseForge Changelog tab.
